Understanding APR vs Interest Rate

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes both the interest rate AND fees, giving you the true cost of borrowing. The nominal interest rate alone doesn't tell the full story.

Key Differences:

  • Interest Rate: The percentage of principal charged as interest annually
  • APR: Interest rate plus all fees, expressed as an annual percentage
  • Example: A 5% interest rate with $200 in fees becomes a higher APR

Why APR Matters:

APR allows you to compare loans accurately. Two loans with different interest rates and fees can be compared on equal footing using APR.

Typical Fees Included in APR:

  • Origination fees
  • Documentation fees
  • Processing fees
  • Application fees

Compounding Frequency Impact:

How often interest is calculated affects the final amount paid. More frequent compounding results in higher total interest. Daily compounding typically results in the highest total interest paid.
