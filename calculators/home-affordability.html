Understanding Home Affordability

Home affordability depends on multiple factors including income, existing debts, credit score, and down payment amount. Lenders typically use debt-to-income ratios to determine loan approval.

Key Affordability Metrics

  • Debt-to-Income Ratio: Keep below 43% (28% for housing only)
  • Down Payment: 20% avoids PMI, but 3-5% options available
  • Credit Score: Higher scores get better interest rates
  • Closing Costs: Budget 2-5% of home price
  • Property Taxes: Vary by location, 0.3-2% of home value annually

Hidden Homeownership Costs

  • Property taxes and insurance (often $300-1000+ monthly)
  • HOA fees (if applicable)
  • Maintenance and repairs (budget 1% of home value annually)
  • Utilities and services
  • PMI (if down payment less than 20%)

Home Buying Tips

  • Get pre-approved before house hunting
  • Budget for down payment, closing costs, and inspections
  • Factor in future maintenance and property tax increases
  • Consider your long-term financial goals
  • Leave room in your budget for emergencies and other expenses
