Understanding Closing Costs

Closing costs are fees and expenses charged when finalizing a real estate purchase. Typically 2-5% of the home purchase price, these must be factored into your total home buying costs.

Types of Closing Costs:

  • Lender Costs: Origination fees, points, underwriting, processing
  • Title Costs: Title search, insurance, escrow services
  • Government Charges: Recording fees, transfer taxes, document stamps
  • Professional Services: Appraisals, inspections, attorney fees

Typical Cost Breakdown:

Loan-related costs (origination, underwriting) typically comprise 50-60% of total closing costs. Title and insurance services account for 20-30%, with the remainder split among appraisals, inspections, and government fees.

Who Pays Closing Costs?

Traditionally, the buyer pays most closing costs, but this is negotiable. Sellers often contribute to costs in competitive markets. Some lenders offer "no closing cost" mortgages by rolling fees into the interest rate.

Saving on Closing Costs:

Shop around with multiple lenders to compare fees. Negotiate with sellers, consider a lender credit, increase your down payment, or look for a lower-cost title company.
