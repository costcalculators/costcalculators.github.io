Understanding Your Salary

Different jobs use different pay structures. Understanding how to convert between annual, hourly, and other pay periods helps you compare job offers and budget effectively.

Pay Period Types

  • Annual: Yearly salary, common for salaried positions
  • Monthly: Annual salary divided by 12
  • Biweekly: Paid every 2 weeks (26 periods per year)
  • Hourly: Rate per hour worked, common for wage workers
  • Overtime: Typically 1.5x hourly rate after 40 hours/week

Deductions from Gross Pay

  • Federal Income Tax: Varies by income, filing status, and withholdings
  • Social Security Tax: 6.2% up to maximum
  • Medicare Tax: 1.45% on all wages
  • State & Local Tax: Varies by location
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement contributions, etc.

Salary Negotiation Tips

  • Research industry standards for your position and location
  • Calculate total compensation including benefits
  • Consider cost of living in different areas
  • Factor in job security and growth opportunities
  • Document your achievements and value proposition
  • Practice negotiation before discussions
