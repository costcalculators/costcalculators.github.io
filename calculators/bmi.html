What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It's a simple screening tool to identify potential weight-related health issues.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5 - May need nutritional support
  • Normal Weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9 - Healthy weight range
  • Overweight: BMI 25.0 - 29.9 - May increase health risks
  • Obese: BMI 30.0 or higher - Increased health risks

Important Notes

  • BMI doesn't measure body fat directly; it's an estimate
  • Athletes may have high BMI due to muscle mass
  • Age, gender, and ethnicity affect BMI interpretation
  • Consult healthcare professionals for personalized advice
  • BMI is one of many health indicators; don't rely on it alone

Health Recommendations

  • Maintain regular physical activity (150+ minutes weekly)
  • Follow a balanced, nutritious diet
  • Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours nightly)
  • Manage stress levels
  • Schedule regular health check-ups
