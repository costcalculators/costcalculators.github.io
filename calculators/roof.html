Roofing Material Comparison

Roofing costs vary significantly by material. Budget $8,000-$25,000 for an average residential roof replacement depending on material and complexity.

Material Types & Lifespan:

  • Asphalt Shingles: $3-5/sq ft, 15-20 year lifespan. Most common, affordable option.
  • Metal Roofing: $6-12/sq ft, 40-70 year lifespan. Durable, energy-efficient, lighter weight.
  • Ceramic Tile: $10-18/sq ft, 50-100+ year lifespan. Premium, luxury, heavy (requires reinforcement).
  • Slate: $15-30/sq ft, 100+ year lifespan. Extremely durable but very heavy and expensive.
  • Wood Shakes: $6-15/sq ft, 20-40 year lifespan. Attractive but requires maintenance, fire risk.

Roof Pitch Impact:

Steeper roofs cost more to install due to increased labor complexity and safety requirements. A 12:12 pitch (45Â°) roof costs 25-50% more than a flat roof to install.

Removal & Disposal:

Old roof removal typically costs $1-2/sq ft depending on material and roof complexity. Asphalt shingles are cheaper to remove than tile or slate.

When to Replace Your Roof:

  • Curling or missing shingles
  • Algae growth or dark streaks
  • Granules in gutters
  • Sagging roof sections
  • Leaks or interior water damage
  • Roof age approaching lifespan end

Cost vs. Durability:

While asphalt is cheapest upfront, metal or tile offer better long-term value due to longer lifespan. Calculate 30-year costs to compare true value.
