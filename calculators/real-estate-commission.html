Real Estate Commission Explained

Real estate commissions are fees paid to agents and brokers for facilitating property sales. The amount is typically negotiable and varies by market, agent, and property type.

How Commissions Work:

  • Split Commission: Usually 5-6% total, split between buyer's and seller's agents
  • Negotiable: Commission rates are not fixed and can be negotiated
  • Seller Pays: Traditionally, the seller pays both commissions from sale proceeds
  • Typical Split: 50% to seller's agent, 50% to buyer's agent

Impact on Net Proceeds:

Commissions can significantly impact your net proceeds. A 1% difference on a $500,000 home means $5,000. Always negotiate commission rates before listing.

Other Seller Costs:

Beyond commissions, sellers also pay closing costs, transfer taxes, title insurance, and potentially seller concessions. These should all be factored into net proceeds calculations.

When to Use a Discount Broker:

Discount brokers may charge 1-2% commission instead of the traditional 5-6%. This can save significant money on high-value properties.
